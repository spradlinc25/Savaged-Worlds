# Characters and Skills

This section outlines the baseline mechanics for building a character, the mathematical formulas for their capabilities, and how they advance.

## Character Creation Steps

Creating a Wild Card (player character) follows these point-buy steps:

1. **Race:** Choose a race (Human is default, granting one free Novice Edge).
    
2. **Hindrances:** Take up to 4 points of Hindrances (Major = 2 points, Minor = 1 point).
    
    - _Points can be spent on:_ +1 Attribute step (2 pts), +1 Edge (2 pts), +1 Skill point (1 pt), or additional starting funds.
        
3. **Traits:** * **Attributes:** Start at d4. You have 5 points to increase them (1 point = 1 die step). Max is d12.
    
    - **Skills:** Start with 5 "Core Skills" at d4 for free. You have 12 points to buy or increase other skills. Buying a skill at d4 costs 1 point. Increasing a skill costs 1 point per step up to its linked Attribute. Increasing a skill _above_ its linked Attribute costs 2 points per step.
        
4. **Derived Statistics:** Calculate Pace, Parry, and Toughness.
    
5. **Starting Wealth:** Standard starting gear funds are $500.
    

## Races

Characters can choose from various races, each providing specific mechanical modifiers.

- **Android:** +2 to recover from Shaken, ignore 1 level of Wound modifiers, doesn't breathe, immune to poison/disease. Pacifist (Major), Outsider (Major), Vow (Major - serve creator).
    
- **Aquarian:** Aquatic (cannot drown, Pace 6 in water), Dependency (must immerse in water 1/day or suffer Fatigue).
    
- **Avian:** Flight (Pace 12), Keen Senses (+1 Notice), Frail (-1 Toughness), Minor Habit.
    
- **Dwarf:** Starts with d6 Vigor, Low Light Vision, Reduced Pace (Pace 5).
    
- **Elf:** Starts with d6 Agility, Low Light Vision, All Thumbs (-2 on rolls using modern/electronic devices).
    
- **Half-Elf:** Heritage (starts with one free Edge OR Elf racial abilities), Outsider (Minor).
    
- **Half-Folk:** Starts with d6 Spirit, Luck (one extra Benny per session), Reduced Pace (Pace 5), Size -1 (reduces Toughness by 1).
    
- **Human:** Adaptable (starts with one free Novice Edge).
    
- **Rakashan:** Starts with d6 Agility, Bite/Claws (Str+d4), Low Light Vision, Bloodthirsty (Major) OR Minor Habit.
    
- **Saurian:** Armor +2 (scaly skin), Bite/Claws (Str+d4), Environmental Weakness (Cold), Outsider (Minor).
    

## Advancement & Rank

As characters overcome challenges, they gain **Advances**. Advances dictate a character's **Rank**, which unlocks higher-tier Edges and Powers.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|**Total Advances**|**Rank**|
|0 to 3|Novice|
|4 to 7|Seasoned|
|8 to 11|Veteran|
|12 to 15|Heroic|
|16+|Legendary|

**With each Advance, a character may do ONE of the following:**

- Gain a new Edge (must meet requirements).
    
- Increase an active skill that is equal to or greater than its linked Attribute by one die type.
    
- Increase two active skills that are lower than their linked Attributes by one die type each (or buy two new skills at d4).
    
- Increase one Attribute by one die type (can only be done once per Rank).
    

## Derived Statistics

These stats are automatically calculated based on a character's Attributes, Skills, and Edges.

- **Pace:** The character's standard movement speed.
    
    - _Base:_ 6
        
    - _Running Die:_ d6 (rolled and added to Pace when running).
        
- **Parry:** The character's passive defense against melee attacks.
    
    - _Formula:_ 2 + half of Fighting die (e.g., Fighting d8 = Parry 6).
        
    - _Unskilled:_ If the character has no Fighting skill, Parry is 2.
        
    - _Modifiers:_ Shields and certain weapons/Edges add directly to this number.
        
- **Toughness:** The character's passive damage threshold.
    
    - _Formula:_ 2 + half of Vigor die (e.g., Vigor d6 = Toughness 5).
        
    - _Size Modifier:_ Add or subtract the character's Size modifier (e.g., Size -1 reduces Toughness by 1).
        
    - _Armor:_ Worn armor adds directly to Toughness, usually noted in parentheses, e.g., 7 (2).
        
- **Max Encumbrance:** How much weight a character can carry before suffering penalties.
    
    - _Formula:_ Strength die x 5 (e.g., Strength d6 = 30 lbs). (Note: Varies slightly if Brawny Edge is applied).
        

## Attributes

Every character has five core Attributes, rated from d4 to d12.

1. **Agility:** A measure of nimbleness, reflexes, and physical coordination.
    
2. **Smarts:** Raw intellect, perception, and ability to process information.
    
3. **Spirit:** Inner strength, willpower, and charisma. Used to recover from being Shaken.
    
4. **Strength:** Physical power and muscle. Used for melee damage and carrying capacity.
    
5. **Vigor:** Endurance, health, and resistance to disease, poison, or trauma.
    

## Skills (Detailed Mechanics)

Skills are learned abilities linked to specific Attributes.

- **Core Skills:** Every character receives Athletics, Common Knowledge, Notice, Persuasion, and Stealth at d4 for free.
    

### Academics (Smarts)

Academics reflects knowledge of the liberal arts, social sciences, literature, history, etc. A successful roll yields basic information, while a Raise grants deeper insight or related but obscure facts.

### Athletics (Agility) - _Core Skill_

Athletics combines throwing, climbing, swimming, and jumping.

- **Throwing:** Used to throw grenades, knives, or improvised weapons. The range is 3/6/12 (Short/Medium/Long) for most thrown objects.
    
- **Movement:** Used to scale walls, swim in treacherous waters, or leap over obstacles.
    

### Battle (Smarts)

Battle is the skill of military tactics and strategy. It is primarily used for the Mass Battle rules or to analyze enemy troop movements and command structures.

### Boating (Agility)

The ability to safely operate sail or motor-powered boats and ships, navigate waters, and handle naval combat maneuvers.

### Common Knowledge (Smarts) - _Core Skill_

Used for general information, popular culture, and basic world knowledge that wouldn't fall under a specific specialized skill like Academics or Science.

### Driving (Agility)

Operating cars, motorcycles, tanks, or hovercraft. Rolls are generally only required for dangerous maneuvers, chases, or hazardous conditions.

### Electronics (Smarts)

Using, repairing, or modifying complex electronic devices. (Note: Repair is used for mechanical devices, while Electronics is used for computers, sensors, and circuitry).

### Faith (Spirit)

The arcane skill required for the Arcane Background (Miracles) Edge. It represents channeling divine or spiritual energy.

### Fighting (Agility)

Skill in armed and unarmed melee combat.

- **Attack:** A standard Fighting roll targets the opponent's Parry.
    
- **Defense:** Your Fighting die determines your base Parry (2 + half Fighting die).
    

### Focus (Spirit)

The arcane skill required for the Arcane Background (Gifted) Edge. Represents manifesting innate, superhuman abilities.

### Gambling (Smarts)

Playing and cheating at games of chance. Used in the Gambling mini-game to win money or glean information at the tables.

### Hacking (Smarts)

Bypassing digital security, coding, and cyber warfare. A failure on a Hacking roll often triggers alarms or locks the user out.

### Healing (Smarts)

Treating Wounds and diseases.

- **The Golden Hour:** Healing rolls made within one hour of an injury taking place suffer the patient's Wound penalties.
    
- **Mechanics:** A success removes one Wound, and a Raise removes two. The attempt takes 10 minutes per Wound level of the patient. A failure means no Wounds are removed; a Critical Failure causes an additional Wound.
    
- **Bleeding Out:** Can be used as an action to stabilize a dying character.
    

### Intimidation (Spirit)

Frightening opponents to force compliance or cause them to be Distracted/Vulnerable during combat (a Test).

### Language (Smarts)

The ability to speak, read, and write a specific foreign language. If the character doesn't have the Linguist Edge, they must purchase individual Language skills for each language beyond their native tongue.

### Notice (Smarts) - _Core Skill_

General awareness, searching, and detecting ambushes. Used to spot hidden clues, hear approaching enemies, or oppose a character's Stealth roll.

### Occult (Smarts)

Knowledge of the supernatural, magic, monsters, myths, and legends. Useful for identifying creature weaknesses or understanding magical artifacts.

### Performance (Spirit)

Singing, acting, playing instruments, or putting on a show. A good performance can earn money, create a distraction, or improve an audience's attitude.

### Persuasion (Spirit) - _Core Skill_

Convincing others, negotiating, and peaceful diplomacy. The target's initial Attitude (Hostile, Uncooperative, Neutral, Friendly, Helpful) modifies the difficulty of the roll.

### Piloting (Agility)

Flying planes, helicopters, or spaceships. Used for aerial maneuvers, chases, and dogfights.

### Psionics (Smarts)

The arcane skill required for the Arcane Background (Psionics) Edge. Represents tapping into mental energy to alter reality.

### Repair (Smarts)

Fixing mechanical gadgets, vehicles, armor, and weapons. Takes 1d4 hours for basic repairs. Requires tools; doing so without tools inflicts a -2 penalty.

### Research (Smarts)

Finding information in libraries, databases, or physical records. A successful roll usually takes 1d4 hours of reading and searching.

### Riding (Agility)

Controlling mounts like horses, camels, or alien beasts. Allows the character to fight while mounted and perform complex maneuvers.

### Science (Smarts)

Knowledge of hard sciences like biology, chemistry, physics, and geology.

### Shooting (Agility)

Firing ranged weapons like bows, guns, and energy weapons.

- **Target Number:** The base Target Number for Shooting is 4.
    
- **Modifiers:** Subject to range penalties (Short: +0, Medium: -2, Long: -4), cover penalties, and illumination penalties.
    

### Spellcasting (Smarts)

The arcane skill required for the Arcane Background (Magic) Edge. Represents reading grimoires and shaping magical energies.

### Stealth (Agility) - _Core Skill_

Sneaking, hiding, and moving silently. Opposed by the target's Notice roll. Moving faster than a walk inflicts a -2 penalty to the Stealth roll.

### Survival (Smarts)

Finding food, water, and shelter in the wilderness. A successful roll provides food and water for one person for one day. A Raise provides enough for five people.

### Taunt (Smarts)

Insulting or mocking opponents to make them Distracted/Vulnerable during combat (a Test). Opposed by the target's Smarts.

### Thievery (Agility)

Sleight of hand, picking locks, disabling traps, and bypassing physical security. Often requires lockpicks or tools (a -2 penalty without them).

### Weird Science (Smarts)

The arcane skill required for the Arcane Background (Weird Science) Edge. Used to activate and maintain mad-science gadgets and gizmos.
